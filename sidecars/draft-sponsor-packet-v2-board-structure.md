## Purpose

North Star House needs a sponsor packet that matches the way sponsorship actually works for the organization now.

The prior packet draft was a first pitch artifact, not a packet already circulated to sponsors. The goal of v2 is to reconcile that draft with Haley's sponsor tracker, current sponsor history, and the May 7 development meeting discussion.

The core rebalance is simple: small support should still be welcomed and recognized, formal sponsorship should begin at a meaningful floor, and the top tier should retain benefits that are genuinely special.

## Proposed Sponsorship Rule

Formal sponsorship begins at $1,000 in cash or approved in-kind value.

Sponsor tiers may be met through cash, approved in-kind contributions, or a combination of both. In-kind sponsorships count at the agreed fair value of goods or services that directly support North Star House, confirmed by Haley / NSH before final tier assignment.

## Proposed Tier Structure

| Level | Amount | Role | Board Review Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Friend of North Star | $150 cash or in-kind under $1,000 | Supporter lane outside the formal sponsor ladder | Working assumption only. Recognition boundaries still need approval. |
| Heritage Partner | $1,000-$2,499 | First formal sponsor tier | Real sponsor recognition and basic event access, without naming/control perks. |
| Preservation Leader | $2,500-$4,999 | Middle sponsor tier | Stronger recognition and more event access than Heritage, but still no control perks. |
| Keystone | $5,000-$9,999 | Major sponsor tier | Prominent recognition and limited association; fills the gap before Presenting. |
| Presenting Sponsor | $10,000+ or signature event underwriting | Lead sponsor tier | Only tier that should carry event-level control benefits, pending approval. |

## Recognition Logic

The ladder separates four kinds of value:

- Recognition: public thanks, name/logo visibility, program or website listing.
- Access: tickets, invitations, or sponsor attendance opportunities.
- Association: sponsor name connected to a program, event, or season.
- Control: naming, remarks, press inclusion, banner placement, or event-level prominence.

Recommended rule: lower tiers can receive recognition, access, and limited association. Only Presenting should receive control, unless the board explicitly approves an exception.

## Open Approval Items

- Final ticket counts. Current working assumption is 4 / 6 / 8 / 12, but this should not be printed as final without approval.
- Friend of North Star boundaries. It may receive light recognition, but should not automatically inherit formal sponsor-package benefits unless approved.
- Exact benefit menu by tier.
- Which control perks are available to Presenting Sponsor: "Presented by" naming, remarks, press inclusion, banner/signage, and event-level placement.
- Which v1 draft perks are deliverable, aspirational, or should be cut.

## Recommended Board Decision

Approve the tier architecture as the working structure for Sponsor Packet v2:

1. Friend of North Star remains a supporter lane below formal sponsorship.
2. Formal sponsorship begins at $1,000 cash or approved in-kind value.
3. Heritage, Preservation, Keystone, and Presenting become the formal sponsor ladder.
4. Presenting is reserved for lead-sponsor control benefits.
5. Staff may continue drafting the sponsor-facing packet while ticket counts and exact perks remain bracketed for final approval.
