## Working Rule

Use the ladder to separate four kinds of value:

- Recognition: public thanks and visibility.
- Access: tickets or invitations.
- Association: sponsor name connected to a program, event, or season.
- Control: naming, remarks, press, banner placement, or event-level prominence.

Lower tiers can receive recognition, access, and limited association. Only Presenting should receive control, and exact control benefits are pending approval.

Important framing: Packet v1 was a draft pitch artifact, not a packet already shown to sponsors. V2 is therefore not undoing public commitments; it is designing the first usable sponsor packet by reconciling the draft packet with Haley's existing sponsor reality.

The user read is that v1 missed the practical ~$1,000 sponsor floor and gave too much value too low in the ladder. V2 should make room for meaningful $1k+ sponsors while reserving genuinely special benefits for the top tier.

Cleared rule: sponsor tiers may be met through cash, approved in-kind value, or a combination of both. In-kind sponsorships count at the agreed fair value of goods or services that directly support North Star House, confirmed by Haley / NSH before final tier assignment.

Cleared rule: formal sponsorship begins at $1,000 in cash or approved in-kind value.

Working assumption, still open: Friend of North Star sits outside the formal sponsor ladder as a light-recognition supporter lane. It may receive thank-you / Friends-list / event-context recognition, but should not automatically receive the formal sponsor package benefits unless approved.

Cleared tier: Heritage Partner is the first formal sponsor tier at $1,000-$2,499. It carries real sponsor recognition and basic event access, without event naming or lead-sponsor control perks.

Cleared tier: Preservation Leader remains the middle sponsor tier at $2,500-$4,999. It carries stronger recognition and more event access than Heritage Partner, but still no naming rights, remarks, press inclusion, or lead-sponsor control.
